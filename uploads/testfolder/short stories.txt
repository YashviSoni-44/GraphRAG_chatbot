The Ant and the Grasshopper: A grasshopper spends the summer singing and playing while the ant works hard to store food. When winter comes, the grasshopper is starving and seeks help from the ant, but the ant refuses, having prepared for the season. This story teaches the value of hard work and planning for the future. 

The Tortoise and the Hare: A speedy hare challenges a slow tortoise to a race. The hare, overconfident in his speed, takes a nap, allowing the tortoise to win with his consistent, slow pace. This story highlights that slow and steady can win the race.
 
The Boy Who Cried Wolf: A shepherd boy repeatedly fools villagers by falsely crying "Wolf!" to get their attention. When a real wolf attacks, his cries are ignored, and his sheep are harmed. This story shows the consequences of dishonesty and losing trust.
